about me

I'm a writer, musician, amateur photographer, occasional artist, yoga teacher, British citizen, now living in New York City. I still drink tea consistently througout the day, but I also eat bagels three times a week, so I consider myself fully assimilated to NYC life.

My first foray in building websites was back in the early 2000s, when I poured my heart and soul into a Geocities site for my teenage rock band. We never got as far as recording or writing any music, but we had a killer website.

Shortly after, I studied IT at A-Level, and, shocked that formally studying IT was in fact nothing like building a Geocities website, I failed miserably, and concluded that the web just wasn't for me.

Fast forward to 2017, when I stumbled upon Khan Academy's 'Hour of Code'. I enjoyed it a lot, so I dabbled further in the "Intro to HTML/CSS" course. I was hooked, and decided to invest time more seriously, so I joined Team Treehouse and began working through the Front End Web Development Track, where I learned more about HTML and CSS, plus basic Javascript, CSS Flexbox, Bootstrap, responsive design, Ajax basics, and website optimisation.

More recently, I solidified my knowledge using resources at freecodecamp.org and codeacademy.com.

I also recently completed the Practical Javascript course at watchandcode.com.

Currently I'm working on learning more about php and MySql.

What I love about coding is that it's based on language. I feel like coding draws upon my passions of both writing and art; the components of the web are like characters in a story, and the code is how characters live out their plot and tell their story to the reader.

It's poetry, and logic, and problem-solving, and I continue to be excited and challenged on my web development journey.

But no, I still can't centre something using CSS without researching it for two hours on stackoverflow.
